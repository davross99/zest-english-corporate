The half-second pause before the right word — gone.

You know English. You speak it well. But somewhere in almost every sentence there's a small decision — in front of or near, above or over — and for half a second you're not sure which one is right. That hesitation isn't a lack of ability. It's one of the most common gaps in an otherwise fluent speaker, and it happens to be exactly the kind of thing a listener notices. Zest English works alongside your students, or your team, to close that gap — until choosing the right word stops being a decision at all.

Close the last gap between what you know and how you sound

Prepositions are some of the most frequent words in English — they show up in almost every sentence you speak. Get one wrong, and it rarely changes what you meant. But it's often the one thing a listener holds onto, and it's enough to make them second-guess everything else you said. That's not fair, but it's how listening works — and in business, where trust in the message matters as much as the message itself, it matters even more.

Zest English doesn't start from what's missing. It starts from what's already there — the vocabulary, the fluency, the effort it took to get this far — and works alongside your students or your team to close the last, smallest gaps, until the right word stops being a choice at all. Not a fix. A finish.

Method

How it works

Simple mechanics, rigorous underneath.

CEFR A2–B1 aligned

Every scene and gap is built to match the level where preposition errors are most common and most costly to confidence.

Zero-ambiguity gaps

Every gap has exactly one defensible correct answer. No trick questions, no partial credit, no debate about what's "close enough."

Repetition, not theory

Learners build accuracy through short, repeated practice inside a story — not through memorising rules that don't hold up in real conversation.
