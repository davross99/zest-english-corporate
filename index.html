It's a basic word. That's exactly why it costs so much.

Prepositions aren't an advanced skill — they're one of the most basic parts of English, which is exactly why not having them automatic matters so much. You use them in nearly every sentence you speak. When they're shaky, it's hard to feel sure of your own English. And when the basics are shaky, it's harder for the person listening to feel sure of what you're telling them.

That's what Zest English works on, together with the learner — not more rules to memorise, but short, repeated practice until the right word becomes a reflex. Not fixing a flaw. Building the fluency that was already most of the way there.

Method

How it works

Simple mechanics, rigorous underneath.

CEFR A2–B1 aligned

Every scene and gap is built to match the level where preposition errors are most common and most costly to confidence.

Zero-ambiguity gaps

Every gap has exactly one defensible correct answer. No trick questions, no partial credit, no debate about what's "close enough."

Repetition, not theory

Learners build accuracy through short, repeated practice inside a story — not through memorising rules that don't hold up in real conversation.
