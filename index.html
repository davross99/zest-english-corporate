Master prepositions and you've taken a giant step towards mastering English.

Prepositions are some of the most frequent words in English, and among the last to become automatic — even for fluent speakers. In or at, above or over, in front of or near. Zest English turns them into reflex through short, focused practice, so the right word stops being a decision and starts being second nature.

This isn't about ability. It's about how the brain learns a language.

Words that carry meaning — nouns, verbs, adjectives — tend to stick early. Small connecting words like prepositions don't carry meaning on their own; they only make sense in context, and the brain needs many repetitions before they become automatic. That's true for every learner, at every level of fluency.

Until that repetition happens, there's a brief pause while the brain chooses. On a call, in a meeting, in front of a client, that pause is heard — not as a lack of ability, but as a completely normal, temporary part of how a second language settles in.

Repetition is what turns knowledge into reflex.

Zest English is built on that same principle: short, story-based practice, repeated often, that gives the brain what it needs to move a preposition from something you have to think about to something you don't.

Once that happens, the pause disappears — not because someone tried harder, but because the word simply doesn't need to be searched for anymore. What's left is fluency: the sentence keeps its rhythm, and what comes through is confidence.

Method

How it works

Simple mechanics, rigorous underneath.

CEFR A2–B1 aligned

Every scene and gap is built to match the level where preposition errors are most common and most costly to confidence.

Zero-ambiguity gaps

Every gap has exactly one defensible correct answer. No trick questions, no partial credit, no debate about what's "close enough."

Repetition, not theory

Learners build accuracy through short, repeated practice inside a story — not through memorising rules that don't hold up in real conversation.

Built on what the research shows

Peer-reviewed research on game-based English learning found that learners using interactive games showed significantly greater improvement — and more confidence and willingness to speak — than learners taught with traditional methods alone. Short, repeated, game-based practice is what moves a language skill from "known" to "automatic" — which is exactly the gap Zest English is built to close.
